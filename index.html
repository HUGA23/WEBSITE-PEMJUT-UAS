INSTRUMENTATION PHYSICS

Instrumentation physics is a branch of physics that focuses on the design, development, and application of tools (instruments) to measure, monitor, and control physical phenomena. It combines the principles of physics, electronic engineering, control, and computing to produce accurate and reliable measurement systems. This specialization studies instrumentation, electronics, and analyzes, modifies and develops tools that can support industrial needs by programming.

SENSOR, ACTUATOR, AND EMBEDDED SYSTEM

[3rd Year of College] - [6 Months to Completion]

UIN JAKARTA

Sensors, actuators, and embedded systems is an important part of modern electronics and control systems. Sensors are devices used to detect changes in the physical environment such as temperature, light, pressure, or motion, and convert them into electrical signals that can be read by electronic systems. Meanwhile, actuators act as devices that respond to the electrical signals by producing a physical action, such as the movement of a motor, the sound of a buzzer, or the switching on of a light. In embedded systems, sensors are used to collect data, microcontrollers process the data based on a specific program, and then give commands to actuators to perform actions. Learning this topic trains an understanding of the interaction between hardware and software in automation,
